        \textbf{Answer:}%
        Yes, you can use the `plural` package in LaTeX to automatically generate the plural form of a word. Here's an example:
        \begin{lstlisting}[language=latex]
        \usepackage{plural}
        ...
        \pluralsuffix{word}{words}
        ...
        Some text with \word{} and \words{}.
        \end{lstlisting}
        The `\pluralsuffix` command takes two arguments: the singular form and the plural form of the word. In this example, if you replace `\word{}` with a word like "book", it will automatically turn into "books" when typeset.
