Quantum physics, also known as quantum mechanics, is a branch of physics that deals with the behavior of matter and energy at the atomic and subatomic level. It was developed in the early 20th century to explain phenomena that classical physics could not account for, such as the behavior of electrons in atoms and the nature of light.

At its core, quantum mechanics is based on several key principles and concepts that differ significantly from our everyday experiences:

1. **Wave-particle duality**: According to quantum mechanics, particles like electrons can exhibit both wave-like and particle-like properties. This means they can exist in multiple states simultaneously until observed, where their properties become definite.
